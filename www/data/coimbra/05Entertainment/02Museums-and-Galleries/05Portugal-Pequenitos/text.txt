<p>Opened in 1940, during the Portuguese dictatorship, <em>Portugal dos Pequenitos</em> displays many traces of the nationalist ideology. Despite its name - Portugal for the Little Ones - it&rsquo;s a park that continues to attract tourists of all ages.</p>
<p>Founded by Bissaya Barreto, an eminent professor of medicine at the University of Coimbra, a surgeon, humanist and philanthropist, it became part of the<strong>&nbsp;</strong><a href="http://www.fbb.pt/">Bissaya Barreto Foundation</a> in 1959.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.fbb.pt/pp/en/highlights/"><em>Portugal dos Pequenitos</em></a> stands out as a high-quality display of sculptures and architectural art. Designed by architect Cassiano Branco, the detailed miniature size houses and monuments continue to charm children and adults.</p>
<p>If you want to feel like Gulliver, visit <em>Portugal dos Pequenitos</em> and take a selfie in the mini-UC replica!</p>
