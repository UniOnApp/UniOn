<p>Along <em>Ponte de Santa Clara</em> and on the banks of the river <em>Mondego</em>, <em>Largo da Portagem</em> was a point of passage for those travelling between North and South. Due to its location, this is where taxes on goods passing through the city were collected, what explains its name: <em>Largo da Portagem</em> (Toll Square). There was once a pillory, where now the statue of Joaquim Ant&oacute;nio de Aguiar stands. Popularly known as <em>Mata Frades</em> (Kill Friars), he was responsible for extinguishing the Religious Orders in Portugal in 1834.</p>
