<p><em>Associa&ccedil;&atilde;o Acad&eacute;mica de Coimbra </em>(or AAC for short) is UC students&rsquo; union. It has a very active sports club, and Acad&eacute;mica's sportsmen compete in national and international events. For example, its handball and basketball clubs have competed in professional leagues. AAC members can also receive training to become umpires and referees.</p>
<p>The club's 26 sections/divisions run training sessions, games, matches and competitions across different locations in Coimbra, for example, on the Campo de Santa Cruz (for young footballers), at the UC stadium and gym, and on the stadium S&eacute;rgio Concei&ccedil;&atilde;o in Coimbra outskirts. So make sure to get informed here and check out the list of all the sports you can practice at the AAC:</p>

<p>While in Coimbra, you may also watch a football match between Acad&eacute;mica and other professional football clubs like Sporting, Clube do Porto, Gil Vicente etc. on the Coimbra city stadium. In the 1934/1935 season Acad&eacute;mica entered the 1st &nbsp;League, consolidating the national trophy success in 1939. In 2012, Acad&eacute;mica became Portugal's <a href="http://www.academica-oaf.pt/home/historia/">champion</a> again. In 2016, the decisive goal of the UEFA Euro Cup between Portugal and France was scored by Eder, who had started his professional career in Acad&eacute;mica (2008).</p>
