<p>You can buy <strong>single-journey tickets in kiosks and in ticket machines</strong> at the tram or bus stops. Some vehicles are equipped with ticket machines, but that&rsquo;s not a rule. <strong>You cannot buy a ticket from the driver</strong>. Another type of tickets are <strong>the time-limit tickets</strong> &ndash; you need to predict the time of your ride to buy the right ticket. Available types of tickets: 10-minute, 40-minute, 24-hour, 48-hour, 72-hour and 7-days ticket.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Another possibility is buying <strong>a ticket on your mobile phone in specially designed applications</strong> </p>
<p>CallPay (<a href="http://callpay.pl/">http://callpay.pl/</a>), moBILET (<a href="http://www.mobilet.pl/">http://www.mobilet.pl/</a>), mPay (<a href="https://www.mpay.pl/">https://www.mpay.pl/</a>) and SkyCash (<a href="http://www.skycash.com/">http://www.skycash.com/</a>). </p>
<p><strong>Ticket inspection is allowed in public transport by controllers</strong> who have special identification tags with their photograph. Ticket inspectors are colloquially called &lsquo;kanary&rsquo; </p>
<p>All the information about the types of tickets, their prices, the PEKA card and the tPurse can be found at <a href="http://www.peka.poznan.pl/web/portal">http://www.peka.poznan.pl/web/portal</a></p>
<p>You can also check the schedule of the bus or tram line that interests you.</p>
