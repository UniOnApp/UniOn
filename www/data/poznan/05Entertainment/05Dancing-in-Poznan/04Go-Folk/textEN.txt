<p><strong><em>Wielkopolanie&nbsp;</em>Folk Dance Group</strong></p>
<p>Interested in <strong>folk culture</strong>? <strong>Appreciate the various aspects of local heritage</strong>? Want to <strong>preserve pieces of forgotten tradition</strong>? The <em>Wielkopolanie </em>folk ensemble (also known as <em>Cepelia</em>, the group cooperating with AMU) will <strong>guarantee</strong> you a <strong>dance trip back in time</strong> &ndash; you will learn dances and folk songs, find more about colorful outfits, and the specifics of <strong>Greater Poland traditions</strong>: dance stories about <strong><em>Podkoziołek</em></strong> [the last day of the carnival), the <strong>unveiling and uncapping ceremony</strong> among the Bamber peasants, the urban folklore, or <strong>harvest customs</strong>. You&rsquo;ll learn national dances like the polonaise, the mazurka, the kujawiak, the oberek and the krakowiak. You can recreate the history of folk fashion &ndash; notice the distinctiveness of Łowicki, Kujawski, Szamotulski, or Rzeszowski outfits.</p>
