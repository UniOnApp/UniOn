<p>A unique collection of <strong>coffin portraits </strong>(16th - 18th c.)? A <strong>stolen and retrieved painting by Claude Monet? </strong>A symbolic history of the nation depicted in the art of Jacek Malczewski? The exclusivity of Safonisba Anguissola&rsquo;s <em>The Chess Game</em>? Founded in 1850s, the National Museum in Poznań gives you an opportunity to see the collections of ancient, medieval, 16<sup>th</sup> to 18<sup>th</sup> c. and modern art, as well as a gallery of posters and design. <strong>Avant-garde art</strong>? Take a look at the paintings by Tadeusz Kantor, Jerzy Nowosielski, or Alina Szapocznikow&rsquo;s sculptures. In the <strong>Print Room</strong> you will find the old prints by D&uuml;rer, Ribera, Canaletto, and Piranesi. When you visit the museum you can also learn calligraphy. For a short moment you can turn into a historian and an art critique. You will find out how to look to perceive the depicted details. If your family with small children drops by, you can propose the museum game <em>The Bag of Museum Hunters</em>. A <strong>date in front of a painting</strong>? The Night of the Museums in the National Museum? A lesson about artistic perception? Ethnographic expositions? <strong>The one and only Museum of Musical Instruments</strong> <strong>in Poland</strong>? Fancy a trip to the out-of-town branches of the museum? Rogalin Palace Museum in Rogalin, Gołuch&oacute;w Castle Museum, and Adam Mickiewicz Museum await you.</p>
