<p>Under the watchful eyes of the billy goats peeking through the windows of the R<strong>enaissance tenement house, the seat of the museum</strong>, you can face a <strong>sweet challenge</strong> and prepare a <strong>St. Martin croissant</strong> according to a traditional recipe. The croissants owe their unique taste to the white poppy seed filling. While the croissants are made, the pastry chefs tell the stories about the tradition of baking the sweets and the show ends with croissant tasting. Don&rsquo;t miss out on the opportunity to taste a croissant as they are protected by the European Law and can <strong>only</strong> be made <strong>in the Greater Poland region</strong>. </p>
