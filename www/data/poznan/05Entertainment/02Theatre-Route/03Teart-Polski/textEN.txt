<p>The Polish Theater in Pozna≈Ñ was founded in the 19th c., in the period of the Partitions of Poland, and is the oldest theater in Poland. The theater was opened in 1875, designed as a haven of Polishness, a sign of protest against the Prussian domination. The first spectacle staged in the theater was <em>Zemsta</em> by Aleksander Fredro.</p>
<p><strong>It is the oldest theater in Poland. </strong></p>
<p>The theater comprises the Main Stage, Malarnia Stage, Gallery Stage and the <em>Basement under the Stage</em>. </p>
<p>In addition to the numerous theater plays in the repertoire, the place proposes other attractions. Among others, visitors can take on a Theater Stroll to find more about behind the scenes. Also, there is the <strong>Intermission Orchestra of Polish Theater</strong>, which prepares concerts. What is more, the theater has something in store for the children, too, for instance <strong>Teatranki</strong> &ndash; i.e. theater games for children and their parents. During these meetings the participants create puppets, mascots, dummies, or stage outfits.&nbsp;</p>
