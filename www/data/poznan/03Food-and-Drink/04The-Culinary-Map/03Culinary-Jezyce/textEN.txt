<p><strong>Jeżyce</strong>, one of the biggest districts in Poznań, rich in history and even richer in its gastronomic repertoire, is now experiencing its culinary Renaissance. Apart from the <strong>Jeżyce Market Square</strong>, where you&rsquo;ll find <strong>an open-air vegetable and fruit market</strong>, many original restaurants in the district are worth visiting. Vegetarians, vegans, devotees of organic food, enthusiasts of meat, traditional, international, or oriental cuisines can find their favorite tastes here.</p>
<p><strong>Organic restaurants/ healthy food:</strong></p>
<p><strong>Specific place</strong>: <em>Juice Drinkers</em>, 8 Dąbrowskiego st, 6 Fredry st. <a href="http://www.facebook.com/juicedrinkers">www.facebook.com/juicedrinkers</a> </p>
<p>Healthy food aficionados will love this place for sure! Freshly squeezed vegetable and fruit juices and healthy sandwiches are the specialty of this place. Perfect for a fresh snack in-between classes. Prices up to 10 PLN.</p>
<p><strong>Specific place</strong>: bistro <em>Marchewkowe Pole</em>, 62 Dąbrowskiego st. <br /> www.marchewkowepole.com<br /> <a href="http://www.facebook.com/MarchewkowePoleCom">www.facebook.com/MarchewkowePoleCom</a></p>
<p><em>Marchewkowe Pole </em>offers fresh soups cooked each day, starting from 5 a.m. On the menu are six soups: three are always available (sour rye soup, carrot soup and letcho), while the rest are seasonal dishes. Prices start from 5 PLN.</p>
<p><strong>Polish cuisine:</strong></p>
<p><strong>Specific place</strong>: <em>Pyra Bar</em>, 13 Szamarzewskiego st.<br /> www.pyrabar.pl<br /> <a href="http://www.facebook.com/pyrabar">www.facebook.com/pyrabar</a></p>
<p><strong>Did you know that Wielkopolans eat the most potatoes in comparison to the inhabitants of other regions in Poland?</strong></p>
<p>Not surprisingly, potato is the dominant vegetable in regional cuisine and there is even a regionalism for potato - in Poznań it is known as <em>pyra</em>. <em>Pyra bar </em>offers much more than just potato-based dishes, with simple, tasty and cheap meals such as soups, dumplings, and pierogi that can be found on the menu. Prices up to 10 PLN.&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Specific place</strong>: <em>Pierogarnia Jeżycka</em>, 37 Szamarzewskiego st.<br /> <a href="http://www.facebook.com/pages/Pierogarnia-Je%25C5%25BCycka/361319767303723">www.facebook.com/pages/Pierogarnia-Je%C5%BCycka/361319767303723</a></p>
<p>If you like the most iconic Polish food <em>pierogi</em>, you must visit one of the finest spots serving the hand-made dumplings with various filling from classic to more exotic flavors. Prices start from 11 PLN. </p>
