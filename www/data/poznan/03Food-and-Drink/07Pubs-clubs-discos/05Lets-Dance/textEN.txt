<p><strong>If you enjoy dancing</strong>, you won&rsquo;t get bored in Poznań. The clubs have <strong>smaller or larger dance floors</strong>, so you just need to find your favorite tune. They have something for everyone: electro music, Latin rhythms, contemporary, hip-hop, live concerts, you name it. You need to be 18 to enter, though. Depending on the place, the admission might or might not be free. Coatrooms are mandatory in many clubs, some of them enforce a dress code. Many clubs feature students&rsquo; nights, for example student Thursdays. You can find more information at Poznań ESN&rsquo;s website: <a href="http://uam.esn.pl/en">http://uam.esn.pl/en</a></p>

<p><strong>A typical Polish drink is&hellip; </strong></p>
<p>the vodka <strong><em>Żubr&oacute;wka</em></strong> with apple juice. But of course, everything is in the skilled hands of a bartender. </p>
<p><strong>Feeling famished after the party?</strong></p>
<p><strong>Try the Polish fast-food &ndash; <em>zapiekanka</em></strong> [baked casserole]. It is a baguette with white mushrooms and toasted cheese. You can add extra salad or sauces. Arguably the best hunger-killing food after a Saturday night fever.</p>
