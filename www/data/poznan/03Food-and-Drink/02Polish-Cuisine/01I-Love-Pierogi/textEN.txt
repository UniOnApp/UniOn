<p>Over time, as a result of various historical circumstances, Polish cuisine has evolved and has been influenced by Western, Central, Eastern European and Jewish cuisines. It can be characterized by <strong>richness in meat</strong> (poultry, pork, beef, game), <strong>a variety of vegetables</strong> (potatoes, cabbage, beetroots), and <strong>herbs</strong>. Many a national dish is made according to <strong>a simple recipe, with flour being the staple ingredient, among others the renowned pierogi (stuffed dumplings)</strong>.</p>
