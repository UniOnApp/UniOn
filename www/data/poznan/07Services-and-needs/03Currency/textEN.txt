<p><strong>The valid currency </strong>in Poland is the<strong> Polish zloty. One zloty equals 100 groszy. </strong>You can come across two <strong>abbreviations designating</strong> Polish currency:</p>
<p>zł and PLN.</p>
<p><strong>Coins in circulation</strong>:</p>
<p>1 grosz, 2 grosze, 5 groszy, 10 groszy, 20 groszy, 50 groszy,</p>
<p>1 złoty, 2 złote, 5 złotych</p>
<p><strong>Notes in circulation</strong>:</p>
<p>10, 20, 50, 100, 200, 500 złotych</p>
<p><strong>1 PLN is worth circa 0,2 EUR. You can check the current exchange rates </strong>on the NBP website (central bank of the Republic of Poland).</p>
<p><strong>You can exchange currency at exchange counters </strong>(quite a few of them are in the downtown and in shopping malls), in banks, or at any post office. Some <strong>ATMs allow you to withdraw</strong> Euro currency.</p>
