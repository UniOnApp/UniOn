<p><strong>Adam Mickiewicz University in Poznań</strong> is <strong>an institution of higher education combining tradition with innovation</strong>, academic autonomy with the willingness to contribute to the local social life, <strong>outstanding researchers with the idea of team work</strong>, <strong>variety of programs with interdisciplinary nature</strong>, fusing regional thought and international mobility. It is <strong>an academic center</strong> employing prominent specialists, educating independently thinking students, <strong>promoting the relationship between master and student, as well as partnership in research, advocating courage to raise unconventional questions</strong>.</p>
<p> In October 2016, on the basis of surveys prepared by StudyPortals and carried out among international students, AMU was awarded <strong>the prestigious Excellent International Student Satisfaction Award</strong>. <strong>Open-mindedness, openness to dialogue, quality of teaching, attractive program offer and interesting projects for mobility students were appreciated and rated highly</strong>. </p>
<p> In keeping with the descriptions published at AMU website, historically &ndash; the University transfers the heritage of the Lubrański Academy to modern times; culturally &ndash; <strong>it avails itself of the unique geographical setting</strong>; demographically &ndash; turns Poznań into a university city; as for investment &ndash; <strong>AMU owns fine historical buildings, which were restored to their former glory</strong>, as well as an architectonically inspiring complex, the Morasko campus. The patron of the University is Adam Mickiewicz, an outstanding poet of Polish Romanticism. The University comprises 15 Faculties: Faculty of English, Faculty of Biology, Faculty of Chemistry, Faculty of Polish and Classical Philology, Faculty of Physics, Faculty of Historical Studies, Faculty of Mathematics and Computer Science, Faculty of Geographical and Geological Studies, Faculty of Political Science and Journalism, Faculty of Social Sciences, Faculty of Modern Languages and Literatures, Faculty of Pedagogy and Fine Arts in Kalisz, Faculty of Law and Administration, Faculty of Educational Studies and Faculty of Theology.</p>
<p> <strong>The strategic goals</strong> for the upcoming years include intensifying international mobility of researchers, creating study programs in partnership with prominent higher education institutions from around the world, <strong>linking the study programs in response to the needs of the job market</strong> by promoting the idea of knowledge-based economy and <strong>supporting academic entrepreneurship</strong>, and strengthening the relationships between the University and its urban surroundings by <strong>promoting education</strong> and the role of academic patronage.</p>
